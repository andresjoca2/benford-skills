# Source Policy

## Principle

Use the cheapest reliable source that can answer the brief. Escalate to paid or bulk sources only when public research cannot produce enough qualified candidates.

## Levels

### Level 1 - Primary Sources

Use first:

- official company or business website
- official professional profile
- Google Maps/business profile
- official LinkedIn company page
- official social profile when it clearly identifies the business

### Level 2 - Public Discovery Sources

Use when Level 1 needs discovery help:

- search engine results
- Brave/search tools
- OpenClaw browser
- gstack/browser-use
- public directories
- associations/chambers
- marketplaces
- local maps and category pages

Directories are usually discovery sources, not final candidates.

### Level 3 - Enrichment And Scale

Use when the brief needs scale, when public search is thin, or when the run has budget:

- Apollo
- Hunter
- LinkedIn search
- Crunchbase or similar databases
- Apify scrapers
- Google Maps scrapers
- other paid data sources explicitly available to OpenClaw

## Final Candidate Rule

Return final candidates that are likely contactable later. For company-first runs this means a company, local business, practice, clinic, agency, store, or person-owned business. For person-as-business categories, the candidate may be the practice/person if that is the market's real buying unit.

## Rejection Patterns

Avoid candidates when:

- they are only a directory and no actual business/person was identified,
- they are outside the requested geography,
- they match a negative signal,
- the evidence does not support the rationale,
- the candidate is too weak just to fill maxCompanies.
