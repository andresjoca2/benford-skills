# OPERATING_RULES.md

## Working principles
1. The test is the main unit of documentation.
2. Differences between auditors should be captured inside the relevant test.
3. Raw information and final operational knowledge should stay separate.
4. SOPs must be written for a junior reader.
5. Uncertainty should be made explicit.
6. Obsidian should not be modified without prior approval.

## Decision rules

### When new information appears
Classify it as one of the following:
- raw intake
- project overview
- domain knowledge
- test-specific source
- test-specific variant
- official SOP content
- open question
- decision

### When two sources differ
Do not force resolution immediately.
Capture both under the test's sources and variants.

### When writing SOPs
- use explicit steps
- define acronyms when needed
- avoid expert shorthand
- make completion criteria visible

### When something is still unclear
Store it as an open question rather than guessing.

## Editing rule
Before proposing changes in Obsidian:
- summarize what would change
- explain why
- wait for approval
