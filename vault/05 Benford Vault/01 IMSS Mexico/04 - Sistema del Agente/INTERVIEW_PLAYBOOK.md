# INTERVIEW_PLAYBOOK.md

## Purpose
Guide how to extract useful operational knowledge from auditors and other experts.

## Interview objective
Do not stop at high-level descriptions.
Push toward concrete operational detail that can later become a usable SOP.

## What to extract
- what the test is trying to validate
- what documents are needed
- what order the work happens in
- what the auditor checks
- what can go wrong
- what exceptions appear
- what outputs are produced
- what parts require judgment
- what is standard versus personal preference

## Useful question patterns
- What are you actually trying to validate here?
- What documents do you need before you can even start?
- What do you look at first?
- What tells you the test is going well?
- What usually causes mismatch or failure?
- What do you do when numbers do not tie?
- Are there cases where this process changes?
- Which parts are always required and which are optional?
- If two auditors did this test, where might they do it differently?

## Extraction rule
The goal is not to collect elegant explanations.
The goal is to capture the real execution logic.

## Output rule
After an interview, convert findings into:
- source notes for the relevant test
- variants if execution differs
- open questions if something remains unclear
- possible SOP content only when the information is stable enough
